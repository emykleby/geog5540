Median Age of Males in Oregon

The spatial distribution of the age group data does not seem to any large scale trends as you go from east to west, or north to south. The only spatial patterns that can be linked to any other demographic aspects could be that many of the larger cities and towns, especially those related to colleges and universities, are located in the NW portion of the state, excluding the westernmost counties bordering the Pacific Ocean. You can see that the age groups for these counties are all in the youngest 2 age groups. This makes sense given the demographic characteristics of these counties/regions.

Professional/Scientific Employees in Oregon

The spatial distribution of this map generally follows a predictable pattern, if you are also knowledgable on the demographic patterns of the state of Oregon. As is evident from the map, there are much more people employed in the Professional/Scientific field in the more populous counties in the western and northwestern portions of the state. Not surprisingly, this is where some of the major cities and towns are located, as well as the major colleges and universities. The larger circles in the NW region of the state are near where Portland and other larger cities are. Going south from there are major university towns of Corvalis and Eugene, which is where Oregon State University and University of Oregon are located, respectively. Somewhat surprisingly, even though the eastern counties are much larger in area, they have a much lower number of these employees.

Median Household Income and % White Population in Oregon

Again, the spatial distribution of this map generally follows a predictable pattern, if you are also knowledgable on the demographic patterns of the state of Oregon. The major metropolitan areas of the state are primarily located in the northwest region of the state. This is where the largest cities of Portland and Salem are located. Going south from there, and a little off the coast, you can find univeristy towns/cities such as Corvalis and Eugene. Considering these aspects, it makes sense that the percentage of white population would be lower in these areas, espcially the Portland area. This also has some correlation with the level of median household income. Some of the highest counties are also located in or near these metropolitan areas. However, there are some counties that are adjacent to these that have both high levels of median household income and % white population. As expected, much of the south and eastern counties of the state have higher % white population. These counties are typically much more rural and relatedly have lower cost of living and lower median household income levels.
